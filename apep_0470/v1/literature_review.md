## Selection Decision (Required)

- **Selected idea title:** The Unequal Legacies of the Tennessee Valley Authority — Individual-Level Evidence from Linked Census Microdata, 1920–1940
- **Why this is not duplicative:** The canonical TVA paper (Kline and Moretti 2014, QJE) uses county-level aggregate data from the Census of Manufactures and Census of Population. All subsequent papers (Kitchens 2014; Kitchens and Fishback 2015; Rappaport 2023) also use aggregate data. No published or working paper uses individual-level linked census microdata to study TVA effects. The IPUMS MLP, which enables this analysis, was first released in September 2020 — six years after Kline and Moretti — and has not been applied to TVA.
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** We construct the first individual-level longitudinal panel spanning three decennial censuses (1920-1930-1940) for the TVA region, enabling individual fixed effects, pre-trend validation on the same people, and decomposition of effects by race, gender, and age — none of which are possible with aggregate data. This reveals that TVA benefits were sharply unequal across demographic groups in the Jim Crow South, a finding invisible in county aggregates.
