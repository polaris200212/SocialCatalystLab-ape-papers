---

## Root Cause Diagnosis

The demand shock in `simulate_transition()` is permanent: `a_t = self.a * (1 - shock_size)` for ALL t. This means employment falls to a new lower steady state driven by the productivity drop — λ has no effect because the economy never recovers from the direct shock.

**Evidence:** Sensitivity table shows emp_48 = -0.019 for ALL λ values (0.01 to 0.14). The paper's inlined table fabricates variation (-0.022 to -0.060).

**Fix:** Make the demand shock mean-reverting: `a_t = a_ss * (1 - shock_size * ρ^t)`. Now λ drives persistence: without scarring, employment tracks productivity recovery; with scarring, workers who lost skills during extended unemployment depress θ even as productivity recovers.

## Workstream A: Model Architecture Fix (code/05_model.py)

### A1. Change δ from 0.034 to 0.023
- With δ=0.034 and f=0.40: UR = δ/(f+δ) = 0.078 (misses 0.055 target)
- With δ=0.023 and f=0.40: UR = 0.023/0.423 = 0.054 ≈ 0.055
- Standard calibration choice (many DMP papers use δ=0.02-0.025 to match UR)

### A2. Add AR(1) mean-reverting demand shock
- New parameter: `self.rho_a = p.get('rho_a', 0.99)` (monthly persistence)
- Change: `a_t = self.a * (1 - shock_size * self.rho_a**t)`
- ρ=0.99 → half-life ~69 months, matching GR housing price recovery timeline
- After 120 months: 0.99^120 = 0.30 (70% of shock has dissipated)
- Calibrate ρ externally from housing data, or estimate as 5th SMM parameter

### A3. Remove scaling hack from SMM
- Currently: model moments are rescaled by (data_peak / model_peak)
- Replace with direct comparison: `gr_m12 = emp_d[12]` (no rescaling)
- With corrected δ and mean-reverting shock, model should produce reasonable magnitudes

### A4. Re-estimate SMM
- **Externally calibrated:** β=0.996, α=0.5, γ=0.5, **δ=0.023**, b=0.71, b_olf=0.65, ψ=0.03, d*=12, χ₀=0.008, **ρ=0.99** (or estimated)
- **SMM-estimated (4 params):** λ, χ₁, A, κ
- **Target moments (6):** GR emp h=12, h=48, h=84; COVID recovery month; SS UR=0.055; SS f=0.40
- If 4-param SMM fails: add ρ as 5th parameter (5 params, 6 moments, 1 d.f.)

### A5. Regenerate all outputs
- Demand/supply paths, counterfactuals, welfare, sensitivity grid
- Expected: sensitivity table now shows meaningful λ variation
- Expected: welfare scarring share much larger (was 1.8%, should be 30-50%)
