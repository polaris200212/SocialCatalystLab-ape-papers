# Research Ideas (Revision of apep_0478 v1)

This is a revision of the published paper apep_0478 v1: "Going Up Alone: Automation, Trust, and the Disappearance of the Elevator Operator."

## Idea 1: Restructured Analysis — Individual Transitions as Core

**Policy:** Automation of elevator service (push-button elevators replacing manual operators), 1940-1950
**Outcome:** Individual occupational transitions via IPUMS MLP v2.0 linked census panel
**Identification:** Individual-level fixed effects + SCM for aggregate validation
**Why it's novel:** v1 centered on aggregate SCM; v2 makes individual-level transitions the analytical backbone, revealing the "Paradox of the Epicenter" — NYC operators persisted longer despite being automation's birthplace
**Feasibility check:** Confirmed: 38,562 linked elevator operators, real data from Azure, all code runs
