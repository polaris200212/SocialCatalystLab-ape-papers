## Response to GPT-5.2's Continuing Concerns

GPT-5.2's primary remaining request is for a quasi-experimental design (building-code event study, strike-based DiD) to identify the causal effect of institutions on automation timing. We respectfully note that this paper is explicitly framed as descriptive economic history — the contribution is in assembling and analyzing a uniquely comprehensive dataset documenting the lifecycle of an automated occupation, not in identifying causal effects. The descriptive framing is now explicit throughout the paper, and all mechanism claims use appropriately cautious language ("consistent with," "suggests," "appears to have preceded").

The paper makes no causal claims that require quasi-experimental validation. It is positioned as a measurement and documentation contribution to the economic history literature, with the racial channeling finding as its most policy-relevant result.
