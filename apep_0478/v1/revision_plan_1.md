# Revision Plan: Stage C Response to Referee Reviews

## Overview

Three external referees reviewed the paper (GPT-5.2: Reject & Resubmit; Grok-4.1-Fast: Major Revision; Gemini-3-Flash: Major Revision). All three praised the descriptive contributions but raised concerns about causal claims exceeding the design's capacity. The revision focuses on calibrating claims to match what the design can identify.

## Key Changes

### 1. Reframe Causal Claims as Suggestive Evidence
- **Introduction second contribution:** Changed from asserting causal effects to presenting "suggestive evidence" with explicit acknowledgment of single-donor SCM and decennial timing limitations.
- **Abstract:** Replaced "triggered a national acceleration" with language about the coordination shock reinforcing local workforce.
- **Conclusion:** Removed claims about national automation acceleration; focused on the paradox itself as the finding.

### 2. Add Limitations Subsection
- New subsection before Conclusion explicitly addressing: (1) single-donor SCM, (2) decennial timing, (3) descriptive nature of individual transitions, (4) denominator confounding in ratio outcomes.

### 3. Reframe Individual-Level Analysis as Descriptive
- Renamed "Displacement Regressions" to "Comparative Transition Regressions"
- Added explicit caveat that these are descriptive comparisons, not causally identified.

### 4. Tighten AI Discussion
- Added external validity caveats about generalizing from a single historical occupation.
- Framed modern parallels as "historical lens" rather than direct mapping.

### 5. Discussion Section Reframe
- Coordination shocks section now emphasizes the paradox (local reinforcement vs. broader change) rather than claiming causal national spillovers.

## What We Did Not Change (and Why)
- **Did not redo SCM at city level:** Reviewer 1 (GPT) suggested county-level SCM, but county-level identifiers are not consistently available across 1900-1950 censuses in our extract. This is a genuine data limitation acknowledged in the Limitations section.
- **Did not add higher-frequency data:** No programmatically accessible annual elevator employment data exists pre-1950. Acknowledged as limitation.
- **Did not remove SCM entirely:** While fragile, the SCM with augmented version and permutation inference provides structure to the comparison. We kept it but with appropriately calibrated language.
