## Selection Decision (Required)

- **Selected idea title:** Voting Their Wallet? Medicaid Revenue Dependence and Provider Political Behavior
- **Why this is not duplicative:** No NBER working paper links individual Medicaid provider billing data to political donation records. The closest work (Kim 2024 Stanford JMP; Jena et al. 2018 BMJ) uses Medicare claims × DIME/FEC but studies the reverse causal direction (politics → clinical behavior). Our paper is the first to study policy → provider political behavior using Medicaid claims, and the first to use T-MSIS data for any political economy analysis.
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** We build the first individual-level Medicaid billing × political donation panel by linking T-MSIS (227M claims) to FEC Schedule A via NPPES, and identify the causal effect of Medicaid expansion on provider political engagement using a DDD design exploiting staggered ACA expansion (7 late-expanding states, 2019-2024). This fills the gap between Branch A (provider politics → clinical behavior) and Branch B (policy shocks → beneficiary political participation) by establishing the missing link: policy shocks → provider political behavior.
