# Late Literature Review — apep_0482 v2

## Key Literature Positioned Against

1. **Bagues & Campa (2021)** — Direct predecessor using same Spanish quota thresholds. Found null effects on aggregate spending. We extend by decomposing within-education and introducing temporal heterogeneity.

2. **Chattopadhyay & Duflo (2004)** — Foundational Indian quota paper finding significant spending effects. Our contribution: explaining why the European setting produces nulls (institutional constraints).

3. **Ferreira & Gyourko (2014)** — US evidence of null effects of female mayors. Part of "European/developed null" pattern we explain.

4. **Besley et al. (2017)** — Swedish gender quota "mediocre man" paper. Complementary — they study politician quality, we study spending composition.

5. **Casas-Arce & Saiz (2018)** — Spanish quota effects on discrimination. Same institutional setting, different outcomes.

## Positioning

The paper positions itself as "explaining the European null" rather than "adding another null." The key insight is that the null result masks opposing period-specific effects that cancel. The institutional explanation (LRSAL) provides a falsifiable mechanism.
