## Discovery
- **Policy chosen:** France's taxe d'habitation abolition (2018-2023) — massive €22B reform with clean staggered design, universe-level transaction data, and built-in placebo (secondary residences)
- **Ideas rejected:** REP/REP+ education zones (geocoding uncertainty), SRU social housing mandate (endogenous pre-reform shares), ZRR rural zones (complex history), rent control staggered adoption (too few treated cities <20)
- **Data source:** DVF (property transactions, 2014-2025, open data) + REI (commune tax rates, 2014-2024, open data). Both confirmed accessible.
- **Key risk:** Anticipation effects from Macron's 2017 campaign announcement could contaminate pre-trends. Mitigation: HonestDiD bounds, monthly event study around announcement date.

## Review
- **Advisor verdict:** 3 of 4 PASS (Grok, Gemini, Codex passed; GPT failed on consistency issues)
- **Referee decisions:** GPT: R&R, Grok: Major Revision, Gemini: Major Revision
- **Top criticism:** No pre-reform data (DVF 2014-2019 removed from public portal) prevents testing for early capitalization at announcement/implementation stage
- **Surprise feedback:** Historical DVF files (2014-2019) no longer available from data.gouv.fr — rolling 5-year window policy. This is a binding constraint.
- **What changed:** Claims recalibrated from "no capitalization" to "no cross-sectional capitalization post-2020"; fiscal substitution reframed as primarily mechanical; euro-denominated power analysis added; prose improved with Shleifer-style opening

## Summary
- **Policy chosen:** France TH abolition — massive natural experiment with universe data
- **Key finding:** Null capitalization explained by fiscal substitution (22pp TFB increase)
- **Main limitation:** DVF data window (2020-2025) starts after reform announcement (2017)
- **Key lesson:** When studying capitalization of announced reforms, data must cover the announcement period. Rolling data retention policies can remove critical historical windows.
