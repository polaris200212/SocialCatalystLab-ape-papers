# Reply to Reviewers — Round 1

## Cross-Cutting Issues

### Running Variable Timing
All three reviewers raise the use of current (rather than pre-2012) second-home shares. We acknowledge this as the paper's primary limitation. Pre-2012 municipality-level ZWA data are not publicly available in digital form. We have strengthened the paper's discussion of this limitation with three mitigating arguments: (1) the ban affects new construction, not existing classifications; (2) 99.2% of municipalities have definitive ARE status; (3) any running variable drift biases toward finding effects, making the null conservative.

### Missing First-Stage Evidence
We add cantonal construction employment analysis showing patterns consistent with expected mechanisms. Municipal-level construction data by type is not available from STATENT at the 2-digit NOGA level for individual municipalities. We discuss this data limitation.

### Density Test (p=0.043)
We acknowledge the marginal significance and discuss its implications more thoroughly, noting that donut-hole specifications (excluding ±0.5, ±1, ±2 pp from the threshold) produce unchanged null results.
