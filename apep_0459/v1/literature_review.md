## Known Related Work (Manual Addition)

**Blair, Heck, Corcoran de Castillo, and Debroy (NBER WP 33220, December 2024): "Tearing the Paper Ceiling: The Impact of State Commitments to Remove Degree Requirements on Public Awareness and Job Opportunities for STARs."**
- Studies job POSTINGS (via Burning Glass/Lightcast) and public awareness (Google Trends)
- Finds 2.5 pp decrease in degree requirements per year of policy exposure
- Does NOT study actual hiring outcomes, workforce composition, wages, or demographics
- Overlap: Medium (same policy, different outcomes)

**Fuller, Raman, et al. (2024, Harvard Business School/Burning Glass Institute): "Skills-Based Hiring: The Long Road from Pronouncements to Practice."**
- Private sector study (not government)
- Finds only 3.5 pp increase in non-degree hiring; fewer than 1 in 700 hires benefited
- Key finding: removing degree requirements from postings =/= actual hiring change
- Overlap: Low (different sector, different question)
