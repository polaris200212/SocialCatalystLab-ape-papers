### Summary

This v2 revision substantially improves on v1 through:
1. **New analyses:** GBP/EUR exchange rate interaction, baseline characteristics controls, shorter event window, département-specific trends
2. **Major prose overhaul:** 24% word count reduction, active voice, eliminated table narration
3. **Honest identification assessment:** Baseline price confounding and German placebo are now presented as central findings rather than caveats

### Strengths
- **Intellectual honesty:** The paper systematically demonstrates both the promise and limits of its identification strategy. The baseline price control result (Table 5) is "devastating" and the paper says so.
- **Exchange rate analysis:** New and valuable. Confirms the sterling channel but also shows Germany responds equally, supporting the composite interpretation.
- **Prose quality:** Significantly improved. The opening hooks immediately, the results tell a story, and the conclusion lands cleanly.
- **Mechanism evidence:** House/apartment heterogeneity and Channel-facing geographic concentration remain the strongest evidence for a UK-specific component.

### Weaknesses
- **Identification fundamentally limited:** The German placebo problem, baseline price confounding, and trend sensitivity are structural — they cannot be resolved without fundamentally new data (buyer nationality records) or a redesigned identification strategy (triple-difference, IV with pre-2016 migration stocks).
- **25 pages exactly:** Main text is at the minimum threshold. Any content additions would need corresponding cuts.
- **Pre-trends marginal:** Joint test p = 0.038 is concerning, even if driven by a single outlier.

### Recommendation
**Publish as-is.** The paper's contribution is the transparent documentation of SCI identification limits in cross-border settings. The mechanism evidence (houses vs. apartments, Channel geography) supports a modest UK-specific component within a broader European divergence trend. Further revision without new data would be cosmetic.
