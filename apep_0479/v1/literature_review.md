## Selection Decision (Required)

- **Selected idea title:** When Revenue Falls, Branches Follow — The Durbin Amendment, Bank Restructuring, and the Decline of the Bank Teller
- **Why this is not duplicative:** Existing Durbin literature (Kay et al. 2014 Fed; Wang et al. 2014) examines bank profitability, interchange revenue, and consumer fees. No published study examines the labor market restructuring channel — specifically how the revenue shock affected bank physical infrastructure (branches), banking employment, and teller occupations. This paper bridges the financial regulation literature with the automation/labor economics literature (Bessen 2015, Acemoglu & Restrepo 2019).
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** We provide the first causal evidence on how a revenue shock to electronic transaction fees reshaped bank physical infrastructure and employment. The Bartik/shift-share design exploiting county-level variation in pre-Durbin large-bank deposit shares, combined with a triple-difference using non-banking employment, isolates the banking-specific labor market effects from concurrent macro recovery.
