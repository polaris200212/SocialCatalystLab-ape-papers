# Literature Review

## Paper: Connected Backlash (apep_0464 v5 - revision)

This is a revision of apep_0464 v4. Literature review was conducted during v1 discovery. Key related papers:

1. **Fetzer (2019)** - "Did Austerity Cause Brexit?" - Closest methodological precedent (causal link between economic policy and populist voting)
2. **Colantone and Stanig (2018)** - Trade shock → populist voting across European regions
3. **Autor et al. (2020)** - China shock and U.S. political polarization
4. **Bailey et al. (2018)** - Social Connectedness Index methodology
5. **Borusyak et al. (2022)** - Shift-share inference framework

## Overlap Risk: Low
The paper's contribution is specifically about NETWORK transmission of carbon tax backlash through Facebook social ties - no existing paper combines SCI with carbon tax political economy in France.

## Delta Statement
This paper contributes the first evidence on social network transmission of environmental policy backlash, using Facebook SCI as network measure and French carbon tax as policy shock.
