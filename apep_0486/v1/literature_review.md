## Key Human Papers (from web search, not NBER metadata)

The NBER metadata search returned no close matches, but the following human-authored papers are directly relevant:

1. **Agan, Doleac, Harvey, Kyriazis & Schechter (2025)** — "Prosecutorial Reform and Local Crime Rates" (NBER WP 34364). Staggered DiD, 35 jurisdictions, UCR crime data + some charging data. Finds reduced misdemeanor charging, no crime effects. **Gap: No Vera jail data, no racial decomposition, no mortality-based outcomes.**

2. **Agan, Doleac & Harvey (2023 QJE)** — "Misdemeanor Prosecution." Examiner design in Suffolk County. Nonprosecution reduces recidivism by 53%. **Gap: Single jurisdiction, no county-level design.**

3. **Petersen, Mitchell & Yan (2024 C&PP)** — "Do Progressive Prosecutors Increase Crime?" CS-DiD, 38 counties in top 100. ~7% property crime increase, no violent crime effect. **Gap: No first-stage verification with jail data, no racial mechanism, UCR-only.**

4. **Ouss & Stevenson (2022)** — Philadelphia bail reform under Krasner. No pretrial misconduct increase. **Gap: Single jurisdiction.**

5. **Fischer & Ludwig (WP)** — DA partisanship and crime. No effects. **Gap: Partisanship ≠ progressive policy adoption.**

- **Why this is not duplicative:** The three closest papers (Agan et al. 2025, Petersen et al. 2024, Agan et al. 2023) all use UCR crime data and none verify the first stage with administrative incarceration data from Vera. The mortality-based outcome (homicides from vital statistics) avoids the UCR reporting bias concern that undermines the existing literature.
