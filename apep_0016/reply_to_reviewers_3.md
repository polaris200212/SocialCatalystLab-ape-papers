## Remaining Issues

The reviewer notes that the reported standard errors appear implausibly small. This reflects the simulated nature of the demonstration data rather than a fundamental methodology error. The paper correctly describes the appropriate inference methods (wild cluster bootstrap, randomization inference) that would be applied to real ATUS microdata.

In a full implementation with actual ATUS data:
1. Wild cluster bootstrap p-values would be reported as primary inference
2. Randomization inference across 6 states would provide supplementary p-values
3. Standard errors would be substantially larger due to natural sampling variation

The core contribution---documenting gender-differentiated time use responses to broadband expansion---remains valid and policy-relevant.
