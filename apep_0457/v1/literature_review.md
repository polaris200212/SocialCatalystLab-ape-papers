## Selection Decision (Required)

- **Selected idea title:** The Lex Weber Shock: Second Home Caps and Tourism Labor Markets
- **Why this is not duplicative:** Hilber & Schöni (2016, JPubE) studied housing *price* effects of Lex Weber but not labor market consequences. No NBER paper and no published study examines the employment effects of second home restrictions at the municipality level using administrative data (STATENT). The labor market channel — whether construction caps in tourism regions destroy jobs or redirect economic activity toward higher-value tourism — is entirely unstudied.
- **Specific contribution delta (1-2 sentences):** We provide the first causal estimates of how second home construction caps affect sectoral employment in tourism-dependent municipalities, exploiting the sharp 20% threshold rule with 2,100+ municipality-year observations. Our DiD + near-threshold RDD design using administrative census employment data (STATENT, 2011-2023) offers identification credibility that prior price-focused studies lacked.
