# Internal Review (Round 1)

**Reviewer:** Claude Code (internal)
**Verdict:** Minor Revision

## Summary

This paper examines the local effects of the 2019 FOBT stake cut on crime and property values using a continuous-treatment DiD design across 279 CSPs. The key strength is the honest reporting of identification failures. The property price result is robust and interesting.

## Strengths

1. **Honest identification**: The paper transparently reports failed placebos, pre-trends, and sign reversal between unconditional/conditional estimates. This is rare and commendable.
2. **Strong property price result**: The -3.8% finding (p<0.001) with clean pre-trends is compelling.
3. **Rich institutional context**: The discussion of crime magnet vs. vacancy vs. displacement channels is well-structured.
4. **Comprehensive robustness**: COVID controls, alternative clustering, tercile specification, dose-response.

## Concerns

1. **Minor**: The CONTRIBUTOR_GITHUB and FIRST_CONTRIBUTOR_GITHUB placeholders need to be replaced with actual values before publication.
2. **Minor**: The abstract is at the edge of 150 words — should verify it fits on page 1.
3. **Minor**: The paper could benefit from a brief discussion of whether the property price effect survives the same placebo scrutiny applied to crime.
4. **Suggestion**: Consider adding property price placebo tests (or noting their absence as a limitation).

## Decision

The paper is ready for external review. The identification challenges are honestly documented, the property price finding is robust, and the writing is clear. Minor issues can be addressed in the revision cycle.
