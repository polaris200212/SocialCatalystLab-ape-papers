# Literature Review — apep_0185 v17 (Revision)

## Parent Paper Context
This is a revision of apep_0185 v16 ("Friends in High Places"). The literature review was completed during the original paper production and is incorporated into the paper's Background and Related Literature section (Section 2).

## Key Literature Streams
1. **Social networks and labor markets:** Granovetter (1973), Ioannides & Loury (2004), Beaman (2012), Kramarz & Skandalis (2023)
2. **Worker beliefs and outside options:** Jäger et al. (2024), Faberman et al. (2022)
3. **Social Connectedness Index:** Bailey et al. (2018a, 2018b, 2020, 2022), Chetty et al. (2022)
4. **Minimum wage effects:** Dube et al. (2010, 2014), Cengiz et al. (2019), Jardim et al. (2024)
5. **Shift-share identification:** Bartik (1991), Goldsmith-Pinkham et al. (2020), Borusyak et al. (2022)
6. **Policy diffusion (NEW in v17):** Shipan & Volden (2008) — geographic/political similarity channels; our paper adds a social network channel

## Novelty Assessment
No existing paper measures population-weighted SCI exposure to minimum wage shocks or tests the scale-vs-share distinction. The policy diffusion test (new in v17) connects labor market network effects to political economy — also novel.
